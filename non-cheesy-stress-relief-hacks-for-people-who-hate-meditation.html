Non-Cheesy Stress Relief Hacks for People Who Hate Meditation

By jesse martin
April 29, 2025


Let’s be honest—meditation isn’t for everyone. If the idea of sitting in silence while trying to "clear your mind" makes you cringe, you’re not alone. The good news? There are plenty of science-backed, no-nonsense ways to relieve stress that don’t involve chanting, incense, or forced positivity.  

Whether you’re overworked, overwhelmed, or just allergic to anything remotely "woo-woo," these unconventional yet effective stress relief strategies will help you unwind without the eye-roll-inducing clichés.  



1. The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique (A Meditation Alternative)  

When anxiety strikes, the last thing you want is someone telling you to "just breathe." Instead, try the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique—a sensory-based method that forces your brain to focus on the present. Start by identifying five things you can see, four things you can touch, three things you can hear, two things you can smell, and one thing you can taste. This simple exercise interrupts spiraling thoughts and lowers cortisol levels without requiring any Zen-like focus.  
  

2. Angry Journaling (Because Gratitude Journals Are Overrated) 



3. Chew Gum Like It’s Your Job

It might sound odd, but chewing gum has been scientifically proven to reduce stress. Studies show that the repetitive motion lowers cortisol levels and can even improve focus. For an extra calming effect, opt for a strong mint flavor—the cooling sensation adds a refreshing distraction from whatever’s stressing you out.  

  

4. Tense and Release (Progressive Muscle Relaxation for the Impatient) 

If sitting still makes you antsy, progressive muscle relaxation (PMR) is the perfect alternative. Instead of trying to quiet your mind, you’ll actively tense and then release each muscle group, starting with your fists and moving through your shoulders, jaw, and legs. The contrast between tension and relaxation sends signals to your brain that it’s time to unwind, making it an ideal stress reliever for people who need to *do* something to relax.  

  

5. The “Sighing” Breath (Faster Than Deep Breathing)  



6. Shock Your System with Cold Water 

When stress hits, a sudden temperature change can jolt your nervous system back into balance. Cold exposure—whether it’s splashing your face with icy water, holding an ice cube, or even just drinking a glass of cold water—activates the body’s "dive reflex," slowing your heart rate and forcing a physiological reset. No ice bath required.  



7. Doodle Like a Bored High Schooler 

Mindless doodling isn’t just for zoning out during meetings—it actually lowers stress by engaging the brain’s default mode network, the same area activated during meditation. Studies show that even simple scribbling can reduce cortisol levels by nearly 30%. So grab a pen and let your hand wander across the page—no artistic talent necessary.  

  

8. Fake Laugh Until It Becomes Real 

Laughter—even the forced kind—triggers the release of endorphins, the body’s natural stress relievers. If you’re not in the mood for comedy, try forcing a laugh until it turns genuine (it works surprisingly well). Or, watch a 30-second clip of ridiculous fails to jumpstart the process. Laughter yoga might sound absurd, but science backs its stress-busting benefits.  

  

Stress Relief Doesn’t Have to Be Complicated 

You don’t need meditation apps, scented candles, or a Himalayan salt lamp to de-stress. These unconventional, research-backed techniques meet you where you are—whether that’s scribbling angrily in a notebook, chewing gum like a madman, or dramatically sighing your way to calm. The best part? They work fast, without requiring a single "om.". So the next time stress hits, skip the meditation cushion and try one of these no-nonsense hacks instead. Your sanity will thank you.
