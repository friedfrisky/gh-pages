The Best Scenic Train Journeys to Take in Japan: A Breathtaking Ride Through Nature and Culture

By jesse martin
April 21, 2025

Japan is renowned for its efficient and extensive rail network, but beyond the high-speed Shinkansen (bullet trains), the country offers some of the world’s most scenic train journeys. These rides take passengers through breathtaking landscapes—snow-capped mountains, lush forests, coastal vistas, and traditional villages—providing an unforgettable way to explore Japan’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you're a travel enthusiast, a photography lover, or simply seeking a relaxing sightseeing experience, Japan’s scenic railways deliver an unparalleled adventure.  

In this guide, we’ll explore the best scenic train rides in Japan, highlighting their unique features, seasonal attractions, and must-see stops along the way. From the alpine wonders of the Hida Mountains to the coastal beauty of Kyushu, these journeys are perfect for those looking to experience Japan beyond its bustling cities.  
 

1. The Sagano Scenic Railway (Sagano Romantic Train) – Kyoto’s Picturesque Arashiyama Route  

One of Japan’s most iconic scenic train rides, the Sagano Scenic Railway (also known as the Sagano Romantic Train) offers a nostalgic journey through the stunning landscapes of Arashiyama, Kyoto. This 25-minute ride runs along the Hozugawa River, providing spectacular views of cherry blossoms in spring, vibrant greenery in summer, fiery autumn foliage, and serene snowscapes in winter.  

Why It’s a Must-Ride: 
- Open-air carriages for unobstructed views.  
- Passes through torrential river gorges and dense forests.  
- Best combined with a Hozugawa River boat ride for a full nature experience.  
- Stops at Torokko Arashiyama Station, near the famous Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.  

Best Time to Ride: Spring (late March–April for cherry blossoms) and autumn (November for fall colors).  



2. The Kurobe Gorge Railway – Toyama’s Dramatic Mountain Pass 

For those seeking dramatic alpine scenery, the Kurobe Gorge Railway in Toyama Prefecture is a bucket-list experience. This open-air train winds through the deep Kurobe Gorge, Japan’s deepest V-shaped valley, offering jaw-dropping views of sheer cliffs, emerald rivers, and rugged mountain terrain.  

Why It’s a Must-Ride: 
- Crosses multiple red bridges over the roaring Kurobe River.  
- Stops at Keyakidaira Station, where visitors can enjoy onsen (hot springs).  
- Stunning autumn colors and snowy winter landscapes.  
- Limited operation (April–November), making it a rare and exclusive journey.  

Best Time to Ride: October for fall foliage or May–June for lush greenery.  


3. The Hakone Tozan Railway – A Switchback Ride Through Mt. Fuji’s Foothills 

The Hakone Tozan Railway is Japan’s oldest mountain railway, famous for its zigzag switchback tracks that climb steep slopes while offering panoramic views of Hakone’s forests, valleys, and (on clear days) Mt. Fuji. This 40-minute journey from Hakone-Yumoto to Gora is especially magical during hydrangea season (June–July) when thousands of flowers bloom along the tracks.  

Why It’s a Must-Ride:
- Features three switchbacks where the train reverses direction.  
- Connects to the Hakone Ropeway for views of Owakudani’s volcanic valley.  
- Stops near Hakone’s famous onsens and museums.  

Best Time to Ride: June–July for hydrangeas or autumn for all colors.  


4. The SL Hitoyoshi – A Vintage Steam Train Through Kyushu’s Countryside 

For a nostalgic and scenic ride, the SL Hitoyoshi in Kumamoto, Kyushu, is a fully restored steam locomotive that chugs along the Kuma River. This vintage train offers a slow-paced journey through traditional villages, terraced rice fields, and lush valleys, evoking the charm of old Japan.  

Why It’s a Must-Ride: 
- Retro steam train with classic interiors.  
- Passes through Japan’s last remaining wooden train stations.  
- Best paired with a visit to Hitoyoshi Onsen.  

Best Time to Ride: Year-round, but especially beautiful in spring and autumn.  


5. The Seven Stars in Kyushu – Japan’s Most Luxurious Sleeper Train 

For travelers seeking luxury and exclusivity, the Seven Stars in Kyushu is Japan’s first deluxe sleeper train, offering a multi-day journey through Kyushu’s most scenic spots. With elegant suites, gourmet dining, and curated excursions, this is the ultimate scenic rail experience.  

Why It’s a Must-Ride:  
- Only 14 suites, ensuring an intimate experience.  
- Visits Beppu’s hot springs, Aso’s volcano, and Yufuin’s art villages.  
- Michelin-star-level cuisine featuring Kyushu’s finest ingredients.  

Best Time to Ride: Year-round, but advance booking (lottery system) is essential.  
  

Final Thoughts: Why Japan’s Scenic Trains Are Unmissable 

Japan’s scenic railways offer more than just transportation—they provide immersive cultural and natural experiences. From Kyoto’s romantic Sagano train to Kyushu’s luxurious Seven Stars, each journey tells a unique story. Whether you’re chasing cherry blossoms, autumn leaves, or snowy vistas, these train rides should be on every traveler’s Japan bucket list.
