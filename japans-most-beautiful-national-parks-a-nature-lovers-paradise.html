Japan’s Most Beautiful National Parks: A Nature Lover’s Paradise

By jesse martin
April 19, 2025



Japan is renowned for its bustling cities, cutting-edge technology, and rich cultural heritage. However, beyond the neon lights of Tokyo and the historic temples of Kyoto lies a breathtaking natural landscape waiting to be explored. The country boasts 34 stunning national parks, each offering unique scenery, diverse wildlife, and unforgettable outdoor adventures. Whether you're a hiker, photographer, or simply someone who appreciates natural beauty, Japan’s national parks are a must-visit.  

In this guide, we’ll explore Japan’s most beautiful national parks, highlighting their key attractions, best times to visit, and why they should be on your travel bucket list.  



1. Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park: Iconic Views of Mount Fuji 

Why Visit? 
Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park is one of Japan’s most famous parks, featuring the legendary Mount Fuji, serene lakes, hot springs, and coastal scenery. This park is perfect for travelers who want a mix of adventure and relaxation.  

Top Attractions  
- Mount Fuji (3,776m) – Japan’s tallest mountain and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Hike during the official climbing season (July to early September).  
- Hakone – Famous for its onsen (hot springs), Lake Ashi, and views of Fuji from the Hakone Ropeway.  
- Izu Peninsula – Stunning coastlines, beaches, and the dramatic Jogasaki Coast.  
- Shiraito Falls – A picturesque waterfall near Mount Fuji.  

Best Time to Visit  
- Spring (April-May) for cherry blossoms and mild weather.  
- Autumn (October-November) for vibrant fall foliage.  

Travel Tips  
- Stay in a ryokan (traditional inn) in Hakone for an authentic onsen experience.  
- Take the Hakone Round Course for scenic views of Mount Fuji.  


2. Daisetsuzan National Park: Hokkaido’s Untamed Wilderness  

Why Visit? 
Daisetsuzan, meaning "Great Snowy Mountains," is Japan’s largest national park, located in Hokkaido. It’s a paradise for hikers, with rugged peaks, alpine meadows, and abundant wildlife.  

Top Attractions  
- Mount Asahidake (2,291m) – The highest peak in Hokkaido, offering breathtaking hikes and hot springs.  
- Sounkyo Gorge – A dramatic canyon with waterfalls like Ginga no Taki (Milky Way Falls).  
- Autumn Foliage – One of Japan’s first places to see fall colors (mid-September).  
- Wildlife Spotting – Look for brown bears, deer, and rare birds.  

Best Time to Visit:  
- Summer (June-August) for hiking and wildflowers.  
- Winter (December-March) for snowshoeing and skiing.  

Travel Tips  
- Visit Asahidake Onsen for a relaxing soak after hiking.  
- Stay in Sounkyo for easy access to trails.  


3. Nikko National Park: A Blend of Nature and Culture  

Why Visit?  
Nikko National Park combines lush forests, waterfalls, and sacred shrines, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s an ideal destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike.  

Top Attractions  
- Toshogu Shrine – A lavishly decorated shrine dedicated to Tokugawa Ieyasu.  
- Kegon Falls – One of Japan’s most famous waterfalls (97m tall).  
- Lake Chuzenji – A scenic lake formed by a volcanic eruption.  
- Senjogahara Marshland – A beautiful hiking spot with boardwalks.  

Best Time to Visit 
- Autumn (October) for stunning fall colors.  
- Spring (May) for cherry blossoms and fresh greenery.  

Travel Tips  
- Take the Irohazaka Winding Road for panoramic views.  
- Visit **Yumoto Onsen** for a relaxing hot spring experience.  

5. Akan-Mashu National Park: Volcanic Lakes and Rare Marimo Algae 

Why Visit? 
Akan-Mashu National Park is famous for its crystal-clear crater lakes, hot springs, and unique marimo (moss balls). It’s a fantastic destination for relaxation and outdoor activities.  

Top Attractions 
- Lake Mashu – One of the clearest lakes in the world.  
- Lake Akan – Home to rare marimo algae balls.  
- Mount Io (Iozan) – A sulfur-spewing volcano with hiking trails.  
- Ainu Cultural Performances – Learn about Hokkaido’s indigenous culture.  

Best Time to Visit
- Summer (June-September) for hiking and boat tours.  
- Winter (December-March) for snowscapes and ice festivals.  

Travel Tips 
- Visit Akanko Onsen for lakeside hot springs.  
- Take a glass-bottom boat tour on Lake Akan to see marimo.  

6. Kirishima-Kinkowan National Park: Volcanic Peaks and Hot Springs  

Why Visit?  
This Kyushu-based park is known for its **active volcanoes, lush forests, and relaxing hot springs**. It’s perfect for adventurers and those seeking wellness retreats.  

Top Attractions:
- Mount Kirishima – A volcanic range with colorful crater lakes.  
- Sakurajima – An active volcano near Kagoshima.  
- Ebino Kogen Highlands – Stunning hiking trails and lakes.  
- Kirishima Onsen – Therapeutic hot springs surrounded by nature.  

Best Time to Visit: 
- Spring (April-May) for cherry blossoms.  
- Autumn (October-November) for pleasant hiking weather.  

Travel Tips  
- Stay in Kirishima Onsen Town for easy access to trails and baths.  
- Check volcanic activity before hiking Sakurajima.  



7. Towada-Hachimantai National Park: Stunning Lakes and Autumn Colors  

Why Visit? 
This Tohoku park is famous for its vivid blue lakes, hot springs, and spectacular autumn foliage. It’s a hidden gem for nature lovers.  

Top Attractions  
- Lake Towada – A stunning caldera lake with boat tours.  
- Oirase Gorge – A picturesque stream with waterfalls.  
- Hachimantai Plateau – Volcanic landscapes and hiking trails.  
- Nyuto Onsen – Remote hot springs with rustic charm.  

Best Time to Visit:  
- Autumn (October) for breathtaking fall colors.  
- Summer (July-August) for hiking and lake activities.  

Travel Tips 
- Drive the Hachimantai Aspite Line for panoramic views.  
- Stay at a ryokan in Nyuto Onsen for a traditional experience.  


Final Thoughts: Why Explore Japan’s National Parks?  

Japan’s national parks offer diverse landscapes, from volcanic peaks and crystal-clear lakes to dense forests and coastal cliffs. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, these parks provide unforgettable memories.
